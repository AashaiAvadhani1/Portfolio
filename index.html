Projects

During a project in my Data Science Class, I was able to develop a deep learning model that found whether news articles are "fake news" by only using the title of article. This was accomplished through a thorough Exploratory Data Analysis to understand how fake news is constructed (word cloud, frequency of keywords, semantic analysis of text) as well as utilizing Google's BERT framework. After encoding words through BERT's pre-trained language model, I trained a Neural Network to learn the pattern and correlation of words together to drive the content/intent of News Article Title's. We were able to classify up to 90% of fake news articles! Link: https://github.com/AashaiAvadhani1/Fake-News-Classification-Methods

As a Freshman, I built a live time based dashboard that produced live sports analytical insights for location data from the NBA. I was able to determine a spacing efficiency statistic that calculated the area of the court relative to the player's and compared relative size with the overall size of the court to gain a true insight on how NBA teams are utilizing spacing on the court. Link: https://www.cmusportsanalytics.com/nba-visualizer-analysis-simulation/ Link: https://github.com/AashaiAvadhani1/Basketball-Analysis-Simulation-Project

During the summer of my Freshman year, I worked with PhD candidates to uncover the reasons why existing deep learning language models are gender biased. In a world of a data influx, we have to ensure that Machine Learning models are proven to be unbiased. I was able to find the key findings in how biased data in text data led to some language models becoming gender biased.
